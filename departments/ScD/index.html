Working in ScD — For Interns & Researchers

The Scientific Department offers diverse opportunities for those interested in understanding the anomalous. Whether you're drawn to experimental research, medical science, or theoretical studies, ScD provides comprehensive training and mentorship from experienced researchers.

What You'll Do

  • Assist with controlled anomaly testing and experimentation
  • Collect and analyze data from research trials
  • Document observations and compile detailed test reports
  • Maintain laboratory logs and research databases
  • Participate in safety briefings and containment protocol reviews
  • Support medical examinations and health monitoring
  • Contribute to theoretical research and hypothesis development

Skills & Experience

Scientific curiosity, meticulous documentation, attention to detail, and strict adherence to safety protocols are essential. Interns and new researchers will receive training in:

  • Foundation research methodologies and documentation standards
  • Laboratory safety and containment procedures
  • Experiment design and data analysis
  • Anomaly classification and containment theory
  • Medical protocols and emergency response
  • Scientific reporting and peer review processes

Getting Started

The Scientific Department welcomes personnel with backgrounds in research, medicine, theoretical sciences, and related fields. Visit the Scientific Zone in the FPZ elevator section to meet with researchers.
