Internal Operations (IO)
Managing the internal operations that keep Site-89 running, the operational backbone of the Foundation.

» Internal Operations Hub «

Internal Operations is the department responsible for managing the day-to-day internal functions that keep Site-89 operational. While the Department of External Operations focuses on the world beyond the site, IO focuses on everything within. From personnel management to facility maintenance, from budget administration to documentation systems, IO ensures that every department has the resources, support, and infrastructure needed to fulfill their mission.

Director: Josiah Beck
The Foundation Behind the Foundation

Every containment procedure, every research project, every security protocol — they all depend on IO managing the systems, people, and resources that make them possible. IO is where operations happen.

Working in IO

Internal Operations does not recruit openly. IO is staffed by senior personnel (roleplay managers or higher) who are hand-selected for discretion, precision, and willingness to make hard calls that keep the site alive.

If you meet the profile, IO will contact you. If you have not been contacted, you are not being considered. Requests to join are ignored and logged.

Operational Warning
IO keeps the lights on, the doors locked, and the records clean. Interference without clearance is noted and remembered. Proceed accordingly.

Why IO Matters

Security secures anomalies. Science studies them. External Operations protects the world. But none of this happens without Internal Operations managing the systems, people, and resources that make it all possible.
