Administrative Department (AD)
Oversees site management, finance, human resources, and policy — keeping Site-89 running smoothly.

The Executive Authority of Site-89

Governance, Policy, and Strategic Direction for the Foundation

» The Executive Branch «

The Administrative Department serves as the executive authority of Site-89, functioning as the strategic and governance backbone of the entire facility. Unlike operational departments, AD operates at the highest level of site authority, overseeing all non-anomalous operations with comprehensive oversight of financial systems, personnel management, interdepartmental coordination, policy implementation, and ethical governance. Members of this department are entrusted with sensitive information and granted broad authority to inspect, audit, and direct the actions of all other departments when necessary to preserve operational integrity and Foundation security.

AD does not run operations. AD ensures all operations run as they should.

» Board of Directors «

Role & Authority

The Board of Directors serves as the collective executive decision-making body for Site-89, responsible for strategic planning, major policy decisions, and oversight of all departmental operations. The board meets regularly to review site performance, allocate resources, address interdepartmental conflicts, and set long-term objectives aligned with Foundation directives. Board decisions carry the full authority of Site-89 administration and are binding across all departments.

Responsibilities

Strategic Planning: Long-term site objectives and operational direction.

Resource Allocation: Budget distribution and departmental funding priorities.

Policy Oversight: Review and approval of major site policies and protocols.

Performance Review: Regular assessment of departmental effectiveness and efficiency.

Conflict Resolution: Mediation and resolution of interdepartmental disputes and resource competitions.

Foundation Liaison: Communication of site status and strategic decisions to higher Foundation authority.

Board Structure

The board comprises senior leadership from all major departments, ensuring balanced representation and comprehensive oversight. Board composition and specific roles remain classified and are updated as organizational needs evolve. Access to detailed board proceedings is restricted to authorized administrative personnel.

» Internal Operations (IO) Division «

What is Internal Operations?

Internal Operations (IO) is the specialized division of the Administrative Department tasked with managing the day-to-day internal functions that keep Site-89 running smoothly. While the Board of Directors handles strategic planning and major decisions, IO focuses on the practical implementation and execution of administrative functions across all facility operations. IO ensures that every department has what it needs, that personnel are properly managed, that facilities are maintained, and that all administrative systems operate at peak efficiency.

Core Functions

Personnel Management & Human Resources

Recruitment, onboarding, records maintenance, scheduling, performance reviews, disciplinary procedures, and personnel development. IO manages the complete lifecycle of employee records and ensures all personnel policies are consistently applied across Site-89.

Facility Operations & Maintenance

Oversight of building systems, infrastructure maintenance, utilities, security protocols, containment facility condition, and operational readiness. IO coordinates with all departments to ensure facilities support operational requirements.

Financial & Budget Administration

Budget preparation and management, expenditure tracking, resource allocation, cost control, and financial reporting to Foundation higher authority. IO ensures all site spending aligns with approved budgets and Foundation policies.

Documentation & Records Management

Maintenance of official site records, policy documentation, incident reports, compliance records, and administrative archives. IO ensures all documentation meets Foundation standards and is properly secured and archived.

Policy Implementation & Compliance

Ensuring all departments comply with Foundation protocol, site policies, and administrative directives. IO monitors compliance, conducts audits, and ensures corrective actions when needed.

Interdepartmental Coordination

Facilitation of communication between departments, scheduling of shared resources, coordination of site-wide initiatives, and resolution of operational conflicts. IO serves as the nexus point for administrative interdepartmental activity.

Why Internal Operations Matters

The Security Department secures anomalies. The Scientific Department studies them. The Department of External Operations protects the world. But none of this happens without Internal Operations managing the systems, people, and resources that make these missions possible. Every document processed, every facility maintained, every budget approved—these are the invisible operations that enable Site-89's primary mission.

Working in AD — For Interns

Administrative Department internships offer exceptional opportunities for those interested in governance, policy, operations, and site management. Interns work directly with experienced administrative professionals, gaining practical knowledge of how complex organizations function at scale.

What You'll Do

  • Assist with documentation, records management, and administrative filing
  • Support HR processes including onboarding, scheduling, and personnel records
  • Help coordinate interdepartmental communications and scheduling
  • Participate in site audits and compliance reviews
  • Assist with budget tracking and financial administration
  • Observe board meetings and strategic planning sessions (where appropriate)
  • Learn site policy, Foundation protocol, and administrative procedures

Skills & Experience

Attention to detail, clear written communication, organizational ability, and comfort with structured procedures are essential. Interns will gain experience in administrative workflows, policy drafting, organizational management, and high-level site governance.
