Governing document establishing the authority and oversight of Site-89

The Constitution of Site-89

Preamble

We, the personnel of Site-89, in order to establish a stable and accountable system of governance; ensure the continuity of leadership; provide for the security, safety, and integrity of Site operations; safeguard against the abuse of authority; and secure the long-term mission of the Site, do hereby ordain and establish this Constitution for Site-89.

This Constitution recognizes decisive executive authority as necessary for survival, while affirming that no authority exists without oversight, documentation, and restraint.

Article I — The Site Director

Section 1. Executive Authority

The Site Director shall be the Chief Executive and supreme operational authority of Site-89.

All Site operations, departments, and personnel ultimately fall under the authority of the Site Director, except as limited by this Constitution.

The Site Director is charged with the preservation of Site security, mission continuity, and institutional integrity.

Section 2. Powers

The Site Director shall have the power to:

  • Direct all Site operations and strategic initiatives.
  • Appoint, suspend, or remove senior Site officials and department heads, subject to constitutional limitations.
  • Declare Site emergencies and exercise emergency powers.
  • Issue binding executive directives.
  • Represent Site-89 to external authorities when required.

Section 3. Emergency Authority

During declared emergencies, the Site Director may temporarily suspend standard procedures where necessary to preserve life, containment, or Site security.

Emergency actions shall be narrowly tailored to the situation at hand.

All emergency actions shall be subject to mandatory post-incident review.

Section 4. Limitations

The Site Director shall not:

  • Chair the Site Oversight Board.
  • Unilaterally amend this Constitution.
  • Suppress or interfere with lawful investigations initiated under this Constitution.
  • Exercise emergency authority for the purpose of personal gain or institutional entrenchment.

Article II — The Site Oversight Board

Section 1. Establishment and Purpose

There shall exist a Site Oversight Board, herein referred to as the Board.

The Board exists to provide internal checks and balances, ensure compliance with this Constitution, and safeguard Site-89 from executive misconduct or systemic failure.

Section 2. Composition

The Board shall be composed of the following members:

  • The Assistant Site Director, who shall serve as Chair.
  • The Vice Chair of the Board, chosen by the Chair.
  • The Director of Security.
  • The Director of Scientific Operations.
  • The Director of Engineering.
  • The Director of Administration.
  • The Chief of Internal Affairs.
  • Up to two At-Large Members appointed by the Site Director and ratified by a majority of the Board.

Section 3. Chair of the Board

The Assistant Site Director shall serve as Chair of the Board.

The Chair shall convene meetings, set agendas, and act as procedural authority.

The Chair shall not exercise unilateral authority over Board decisions.

The Chair shall appoint a Vice Chair of the Board, subject to confirmation by a simple majority of the Board.

Section 4. Vice Chair of the Board

The Vice Chair shall be a high-ranking individual of the Site selected by the Chair.

The Vice Chair shall not be the Site Director.

The Vice Chair shall assist the Chair in administrative and procedural duties.

The Vice Chair shall assume the duties of the Chair in the Chair's absence or incapacity.

The Vice Chair shall possess voting rights equal to other Board members.

Section 5. Voting

Each Board member shall possess one vote.

The Chair shall vote only in the event of a tie, unless otherwise specified.

The Site Director shall not possess voting rights on the Board.

Article III — Powers and Restrictions of the Board

Section 1. Powers

The Board shall have the power to:

  • Review executive directives and Site-wide policies.
  • Mandate audits, investigations, and compliance reviews.
  • Issue formal advisories, objections, and findings.
  • Freeze or delay major initiatives pending review.
  • Initiate impeachment proceedings in accordance with Article VI.

Section 2. Restrictions

The Board shall not:

  • Issue direct operational orders.
  • Override real-time emergency authority.
  • Remove the Site Director except through constitutional process.
  • Appoint a permanent Site Director.

Article IV — Dual-Key Governance

Section 1. Dual Authorization

The following actions shall require concurrence of both the Site Director and the Chair of the Board:

  • Amendments to this Constitution.
  • Creation, dissolution, or merger of major departments.
  • Structural reorganization of the Site.
  • Budget reallocations exceeding thresholds established by policy.

Section 2. Deadlock

In the event of deadlock, the matter shall be deferred or escalated in accordance with Board procedure.

Article V — Departments and Administration

Section 1. Departments

Site-89 shall maintain such departments as required for its mission.

Department heads are accountable to the Site Director and subject to Board oversight.

Section 2. Internal Affairs

Internal Affairs shall serve as the investigative and compliance arm of the Site.

Internal Affairs shall operate independently when conducting investigations under this Constitution.

Article VI — Impeachment and Removal

Section 1. Grounds for Impeachment

The Site Director may be impeached only for:

  • Gross abuse of authority.
  • Willful violation of this Constitution.
  • Severe misconduct or corruption.
  • Deliberate compromise of Site security.
  • Persistent refusal to comply with lawful oversight.

Section 2. Initiation

A motion to investigate shall require a two-thirds majority of the Board.

The motion shall specify alleged violations.

Section 3. Investigation

Investigations shall be conducted by Internal Affairs or an independent body designated by the Board.

The Site Director shall retain the right to respond to all findings.

Section 4. Impeachment Vote

Impeachment shall require a three-quarters supermajority of the full Board.

Failure to achieve the required threshold shall bar renewed proceedings for a defined period.

Section 5. Consequences

Upon impeachment, the Site Director may be temporarily suspended.

The Assistant Site Director shall assume duties as Interim Director.

Permanent removal shall require confirmation beyond the Board.

Article VII — Founding Director Safeguard

The sitting Site Director, as the founding Director of Site-89, shall be subject to a Founding Director Safeguard.

Permanent removal of the founding Site Director shall require external confirmation or extraordinary review, except in cases of immediate and catastrophic misconduct.

This safeguard may not be repealed without dual-key authorization and a four-fifths vote of the Board.

Article VIII — Rights and Duties of Personnel

Personnel are subject to Site authority and discipline.

Personnel retain the right to lawful reporting, due process, and protection from retaliatory abuse.

Article IX — Amendments

Amendments to this Constitution require:

  1. Dual-key authorization.
  2. A two-thirds majority of the Board.

Amendments shall take effect upon ratification.

Article X — Ratification

This Constitution shall take effect upon ratification by the Site Director and the Site Oversight Board and shall remain the supreme governing document of Site-89.

End of Constitution
